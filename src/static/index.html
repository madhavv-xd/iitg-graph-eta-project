Network Operations Strategy Memo

Prepared for: Head of Network Operations Classification: Internal — Operations Delhivery Graph Intelligence System

Executive Summary

A graph-based analysis of Delhivery's logistics network reveals that 84.5% of all deliveries currently breach SLA, with actual transit times running a median of 1.7× longer than OSRM predictions. The root cause is not uniformly distributed — it is concentrated in a small number of structurally critical hubs and chronically delayed corridors.

This analysis identifies the top 5 bottleneck hubs responsible for a disproportionate share of SLA failures, quantifies the revenue at risk, and provides specific intervention recommendations for each hub. Upgrading the top 3 hubs alone is projected to reduce SLA breaches by approximately 20%, recovering an estimated Rs 2.3 Crore in annual revenue.

The network is sparse — most corridors carry fewer than 10 trips. Traffic is distributed across many routes rather than concentrated in a few highways, which means hub-level interventions yield higher leverage than corridor-level fixes.

2. Top 5 Bottleneck Hubs

#1 — Gurgaon Bilaspur HB, Haryana  Score: 0.733

This hub sits on 34.6% of all critical delivery paths in the network. With 49 outgoing corridors and a SLA breach rate of 79.4%, delays here cascade across the largest downstream surface area of any hub.

  • Betweenness: 0.346  ·  Delay: 1.60×  ·  SLA Breach: 79.4%
  • Recommendation: Parallel route addition + capacity upgrade

#2 — Bangalore Nelmangala H, Karnataka  Score: 0.574

Major Karnataka gateway with 35 outgoing corridors. Betweenness of 0.237 means it sits on nearly a quarter of all critical paths. SLA breach rate of 77.4% makes it the second largest contributor.

  • Recommendation: Capacity upgrade + route type shift

#3 — Helencha Colony, West Bengal  Score: 0.505

Extreme delay factor of 11.6× — highest in the network. Every shipment from this hub takes over 11 times longer than predicted. Facility-level problem, not structural.

  • Recommendation: Route type reassignment review

#4 — Mariani Mainroad D, Assam  ·  #5 — Simlapal Central D1, West Bengal

Both show 11.6× delay and 100% SLA breach rate. Two of five top hubs in West Bengal suggests a state-level infrastructure issue.

  • Recommendation: Facility upgrades

4. Recommendations

Immediate (0–3 months)

  • Gurgaon Bilaspur — Parallel route planning. Affects 34.6% of all network paths.
  • Bangalore Nelmangala — Facility audit and capacity upgrade.
  • Helencha + Mariani — Route assignment review, 11.6× delay is facility-level.

Medium-term (3–6 months)

  • Bhiwandi–Mumbai cluster — Highest SLA contribution, dedicated capacity needed.
  • Deploy graph-enhanced ETA model — 15.6% MAE improvement for customer promises.
